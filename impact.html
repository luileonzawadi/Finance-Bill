The Big Picture

Why is the government doing this?

The government is heavily in debt. To pay off these loans without borrowing more from outside, they are doing what they call "Fiscal Consolidation."

In simple terms, instead of just taxing formal salaries, they are now taxing wealth (like cars) and consumption (like bread and plastics) to squeeze more money locally.

Where does the money go? Debt Repayment
Approximately 75% of collected revenue goes to paying national debt.
Legislative Alignment

Tax Legislation & Finance Bill Alignment Update

Technical review of VAT, Excise Duty, Income Tax, and regulatory amendments.

VAT Structural Reclassification

Selected products moved from Zero-Rated to Exempt: mobile telephone handsets, electric motorcycles, electric passenger buses, sugarcane transportation, and solar products. Ordinary bread remains Zero-Rated, protecting retail prices.

Excise Duty on Phones

Imported cellular phones excise duty increased from 10% to 25%. Expected to boost local assembly, increase customs revenue, and raise retail prices for imported devices.

TPA & Road Levy

Tax Procedures Act overhauled for enhanced digital compliance and KRA oversight. Road Maintenance Levy reduced to lower fuel pump prices and transport costs for distribution-dependent businesses.
