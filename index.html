The Mwananchi Summary

What This Means For You

Cost of Living (Gharama ya Maisha)

Taxes like the Eco Levy and 16% VAT on bread mean daily shopping for food, electronics, and packaged items will be more expensive.

Small Businesses (Hustlers)

KRA is expanding its net. If you sell online or run an informal shop, expect stricter tax rules, digital tracking, and higher operating costs.

Car Owners (Usafiri)

The new 2.5% Motor Vehicle Tax means maintaining your car and renewing your insurance will now cost significantly more every single year.

The Big Picture

Why is the government doing this?

The government is heavily in debt. To pay off these loans without borrowing more from outside, they are doing what they call "Fiscal Consolidation."

In simple terms, instead of just taxing formal salaries, they are now taxing wealth (like cars) and consumption (like bread and plastics) to squeeze more money locally.

Where does the money go? Debt Repayment
Approximately 75% of collected revenue goes to paying national debt.
