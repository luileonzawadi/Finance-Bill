Quick Overview

2025 vs 2026 At a Glance

A simple summary of what changed between the two Finance Bills.

Finance Bill 2025 introduced major tax reforms in Kenya. Finance Bill 2026 focused on enforcing those reforms more aggressively.

Finance Bill 2025
  • Introduced reforms
  • Expanded taxes
  • Added new tax systems
  • Focused on revenue collection
Finance Bill 2026
  • Strengthened enforcement
  • Improved digital monitoring
  • Automated compliance systems
  • Focused on enforcement and visibility
Simple Meaning: 2025 opened the door to more taxes and compliance. 2026 tightened control and increased monitoring.

Tax Legislation & Finance Bill Alignment Update

Technical review of VAT, Excise Duty, Income Tax, and regulatory amendments.

Overview

Core Areas Affected

Income Tax

Structural adjustments to both corporate and individual tax regimes.

Value Added Tax (VAT)

Reclassification of products from Zero-Rated to Exempt status.

Excise Duty

Increased taxation on imported technology devices.

Tax Procedures Act (TPA)

Enhanced digital compliance and tax administration oversight.

Infrastructure Levies

Reduction of Road Maintenance Levy to lower fuel and logistics costs.

VAT Reforms

VAT Structural Reclassification

Selected products moved from Zero-Rated to Exempt: mobile telephone handsets, electric motorcycles, electric passenger buses, sugarcane transportation, and solar products.

Bread remains Zero-Rated: Ordinary bread was excluded from Amendment 32. Bakers can still recover input VAT, protecting retail bread prices.
Excise Duty

Excise Duty Adjustments

Category Previous Rate Amended Rate
Imported Cellular Phones 10% 25%
Impact: The 15% increase is expected to increase government customs revenue, encourage local phone assembly and manufacturing, and raise retail prices for imported mobile devices.
Regulatory Changes

Regulatory and Procedural Amendments

TPA Overhaul

Enhanced real-time transaction reporting, improved automated accounting integration, stronger KRA oversight, and adjusted dispute resolution timelines.

Road Maintenance Levy Reduction

Expected to lower fuel pump prices, reduce transport and logistics costs, and provide relief for distribution-dependent businesses.

The Mwananchi Summary

What This Means For You

Small Businesses (Hustlers)

KRA is expanding its net. If you sell online or run an informal shop, expect stricter tax rules, digital tracking, and higher operating costs.

Car Owners (Usafiri)

The new 2.5% Motor Vehicle Tax means maintaining your car and renewing your insurance will now cost significantly more every single year.

The Big Picture

Why is the government doing this?

The government is heavily in debt. To pay off these loans without borrowing more from outside, they are doing what they call "Fiscal Consolidation."

In simple terms, instead of just taxing formal salaries, they are now taxing wealth (like cars) and consumption (like bread and plastics) to squeeze more money locally.

Where does the money go? Debt Repayment
Approximately 75% of collected revenue goes to paying national debt.
